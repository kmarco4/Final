Isaiah Tryee Williams, 19 years old * Las Vegas, Nevada

Isaiah Tyree Williams was a 19-year-old Black male who was fatally shot by police in Las Vegas, Nevada who were serving a warrant for a suspect on a murder that happened in November on January 10, 2022. According to police, Isaiah fired 18 times at officers and between five officers, they fired 23 times in total back at Isaiah. Body cam footage demonstrates the police storming into an apartment that Isaiah was in, and police officers were met with gunfire from Isaiah. Body cam footage showed five SWAT officers, three with rifles, firing a total of 23 shots towards Isaiah as he was laying on a couch while Isaiah fired 18 rounds towards police. According to the mother of Isaiah, he was a heavy sleeper and had missed curfew which is why Isaiah decided to sleep at his friend`s apartment. His mother believed that Isaiah was uncomfortable sleeping somewhere new which is why he with a gun by his side. Isaiah was not connected to the suspect that police were looking for nor did the suspect live in that apartment that Isaiah was in.

Police stated that one officer broke through the door to enter the apartment in which two other officers followed. Another officer fired three shots from outside a window of the apartment where Isaiah was later pronounced dead. In a press conference, police stated that they were not at the wrong address, however, police also admitted that Isaiah was not the right person they were looking for since they had arrested the correct suspect a couple of days after the shooting. During the shootout, two officers were shot, but one of the officers had been shot in both arms and in a leg. According to Sheriff Andrew Walsh, the correct suspect was not in the apartment, but was later found and arrested. The shooting remained under investigation while the LVMPD Critical Incident Review Team focuses on policy, tactics, and training related to the use of force. Tyree Williams died by Officers Kerry Kubla, Brice Clements, James Rothenburg, Alex Gonzales, and Sargent Russell Backman.
